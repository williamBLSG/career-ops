## InsightStack: Modular AI Decision Intelligence Platform

The systems map to a four-layer architecture that defines modern AI platforms like Palantir and AlphaSense — signal acquisition, data integrity, intelligence engines, decision systems.

### Layer 1: Signal Acquisition
| System | Domain | What it does |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **SignalHive** | Market intelligence | Converts large-scale unstructured community signals into structured strategic insights |
| **TrendRank** | Product demand intelligence | Combines marketplace data with search demand signals; calculates Heat Scores for product momentum |
| **AuctionSight** | Asset pricing intelligence | Multi-tier similarity matching for fair market pricing with confidence-based automation |
| **AffiliatePulse** | Revenue intelligence | Aggregates analytics and affiliate platform data for unified performance insights |

### Layer 3: Intelligence Engines

## The Underlying Thesis

The constraint on enterprise AI adoption isn't access to technology. It's the scarcity of judgment — knowing which AI capability belongs in which part of a system, how to wire disparate capabilities into something coherent, and how to deliver it inside the compliance, data quality, and operational constraints that kill most initiatives before they scale.

That judgment comes from making real architectural decisions under real conditions and being accountable for whether the system actually works in production.
